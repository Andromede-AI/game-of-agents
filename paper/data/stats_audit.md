## Bootstrap method (M6)

`analysis/compare.py:_bootstrap_ci` implements a standard percentile bootstrap of the mean (n=1000 resamples, alpha=0.05). No BCa or studentized correction. For conditions with N<=3 this is biased and anticonservative; we disclose this in the paper limitations and tag small-N cells accordingly.

## B1 hostility Wilson 95% CI (M11)

From the deterministic chat-content rerun on the 39-run corpus, we read the exact hostility counts for the two paper conditions named in the text (5.8% B1 hostility vs 39.5% competitive-frame hostility).

Wilson CI using the exact condition totals: B1 = 1795 messages, Competitive = 215 messages.

- B1 cooperative hostility: 105/1795 = 0.058, Wilson 95% CI [0.049, 0.070]
- Competitive framing hostility: 85/215 = 0.395, Wilson 95% CI [0.332, 0.462]

Ratio lower bound: CI-low competitive / CI-high B1 = 0.462 / 0.070 (upper), or 0.332 / 0.070 = 4.7x (conservative). So the ~6:1 ratio reported in the paper is the point estimate; the conservative CI-based bound is ~5:1 even if B1 were at its upper 95% bound.
